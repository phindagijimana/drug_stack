# Further reading

> Books, papers, courses, and online resources to go deeper.

## Books

### Medicinal chemistry

- **Silverman, Holladay — *The Organic Chemistry of Drug Design and Drug Action***. The graduate-level reference.
- **Patrick — *Introduction to Medicinal Chemistry***. The undergraduate-level reference.
- **Wermuth — *The Practice of Medicinal Chemistry***. Industrial practitioner's tome.

### Pharmacology / PK / PD

- **Rang, Dale, Ritter — *Pharmacology***. The undergraduate standard.
- **Rowland, Tozer — *Clinical Pharmacokinetics***.
- **Shargel — *Applied Biopharmaceutics and Pharmacokinetics***.

### Cheminformatics / computational

- **Leach, Gillet — *An Introduction to Chemoinformatics***.
- **Schneider, Baringhaus — *Molecular Design***.
- **Frenkel, Smit — *Understanding Molecular Simulation***.

### ML / data science

- **Goodfellow, Bengio, Courville — *Deep Learning***.
- **Hastie, Tibshirani, Friedman — *Elements of Statistical Learning***.
- **Murphy — *Probabilistic Machine Learning***. Modern, breadth.

### Drug development / clinical

- **Friedman, Furberg, DeMets — *Fundamentals of Clinical Trials***.
- **Pocock — *Clinical Trials: A Practical Approach***.

## Courses

- **MIT 7.81 / 7.91 — Quantitative Genomics**.
- **Stanford CS 273C — Computational Methods for Biomedical Imaging**.
- **DeepLearning.AI — Drug Discovery with ML** (and related specialisations).
- **The Open University — Introduction to Drug Discovery** (free).
- **MITxBio, EdX, Coursera** offer pharmacology / drug-discovery survey courses.

## Conferences

- **ACS National Meeting** — broad medicinal chemistry.
- **AAPS National Biotech Conference / PharmSci 360** — PK/PD and biopharm.
- **NeurIPS / ICML** — ML side, including ML-for-drug-discovery workshops.
- **ICLR — ML4Molecules workshop**.
- **AAIH (Alliance for AI in Healthcare)** — industry-oriented.
- **CHI conferences** (Drug Discovery Chemistry, Discovery on Target, World Cheminformatics).

## Online resources

- **RDKit blog & cookbook** — Greg Landrum + community.
- **Practical Cheminformatics blog** (Pat Walters) — go-to for production-minded molecular ML.
- **OpenForceField, OpenMM tutorials**.
- **Chemprop docs**.
- **DeepChem tutorials**.
- **Open Targets documentation**.
- **NIH OpenScience initiatives** — many free datasets and code.

## Communities

- **Open Source Molecular Modeling discussion list**.
- **rdkit-discuss mailing list / GitHub Discussions**.
- **/r/molecularbiology, /r/chemistry, /r/Mendel**.
- **Cheminformatics-leaning Slack/Discord communities** — change frequently; search current.

## Tracking the field

- **bioRxiv / chemRxiv / arXiv** — preprints first.
- **Nature Reviews Drug Discovery, Drug Discovery Today, J Med Chem, J Chem Inf Model, Cell Chemical Biology** — primary literature.
- **AlphaXiv / OpenReview comments** — community critique.
- **Twitter/X drug-discovery-ML community** — fast-moving and noisy.

## In practice

- **One paper a week**, picked from this handbook's [landmark list](landmark/papers.md).
- **One textbook chapter a month**, on whichever topic you currently work on.
- **One blog post a week**, from someone whose taste you trust (Walters, Landrum, Greene, Bender).
- **One conference talk a year** at a venue where your peers go.

That cadence keeps a working scientist current without burning out.
