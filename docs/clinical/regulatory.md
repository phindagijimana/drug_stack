# Regulatory science

> FDA, EMA, MHRA, NMPA. The minimum any drug-discovery contributor should know.

## The agencies

- **FDA** (Food and Drug Administration, US) — gold standard, sets most global precedents.
- **EMA** (European Medicines Agency) — pan-European; works through CHMP rapporteurs.
- **MHRA** (UK) — post-Brexit; increasingly its own pathway.
- **NMPA** (China) — large, distinct, important.
- **PMDA** (Japan), **Health Canada**, **TGA** (Australia), **CDSCO** (India), and many others.

## The major submissions

- **IND** (Investigational New Drug) — FDA permission to begin clinical trials.
- **CTA** (Clinical Trial Application) — EU equivalent.
- **NDA / BLA** (New Drug Application / Biologics License Application) — FDA marketing approval.
- **MAA** (Marketing Authorisation Application) — EU equivalent.
- **sNDA / sBLA** — supplements (new indications, labels).

## What goes into an IND

- **Pharmacology and toxicology data** — preclinical efficacy and safety.
- **CMC (Chemistry, Manufacturing, and Controls)** — how the drug substance is made and characterised.
- **Clinical protocol** — for the proposed first-in-human study.
- **Investigator brochure (IB)** — summary for the trial doctors.

For a typical IND, 80% of the page count is preclinical safety; 20% is everything else.

## What goes into an NDA / BLA

- **Quality (CMC) module** — extensive manufacturing data.
- **Nonclinical** — animal pharmacology and toxicology.
- **Clinical** — trial results, integrated summaries of efficacy and safety.
- **Labelling** — proposed prescribing information.
- **Risk management plan**.

Total page count: ~100 000–500 000.

## Accelerated pathways

- **Fast Track** (FDA) — designation for serious-condition drugs with unmet need.
- **Breakthrough Therapy** (FDA) — for drugs with substantial improvement evidence on a clinically significant endpoint.
- **Accelerated Approval** (FDA) — approval on surrogate endpoint, with confirmatory post-marketing studies.
- **Priority Review** — 6-month review instead of 10.
- **PRIME** (EMA) — Priority Medicines scheme.
- **Promising Innovative Medicine (PIM)** (UK).

For drug discovery teams: these matter because they change the data requirements at filing.

## In silico / AI considerations

- **Validated computational models** can support sections of NDAs (e.g. PBPK for label calculations).
- **AI / ML medical devices** follow SaMD / GMLP pathways (covered in [AI / ML → Regulatory](../ai/regulatory.md)).
- **Real-world evidence** is increasingly accepted to support post-approval label extensions; harder for primary approval.

## In practice

- **Engage regulatory affairs early.** What is "obviously fine" to a computational scientist may have specific regulatory requirements.
- **Know the FDA guidance documents** for your area — they're free, public, and authoritative.
- **For AI / ML tools**, watch [FDA's AI/ML medical device program](https://www.fda.gov/medical-devices/software-medical-device-samd/artificial-intelligence-and-machine-learning-software-medical-device) — it changes rapidly.
