# Landmark work

> The papers, datasets, pipelines, and atlases the field stands on.

A drug-discovery scientist who has not read the foundational papers will reinvent solved problems and miss known traps. This section is the curated short list — papers you should have read, resources you should know exist.

## How to read landmark work

- **Start with the seminal paper**, not the latest follow-up. The original framing matters.
- **Read the replication papers.** Most "breakthrough" results are dampened by careful re-analysis.
- **Read the negative results.** A failed program teaches more than a successful one.
- **Read across disciplines.** The clinical, chemistry, and ML camps publish in separate venues; truth lives in the intersection.

## A reading order for the curious

If you read one paper per week, the following sequence gives a year of coverage:

1. Lipinski's rule of 5 [Lipinski 1997].
2. The kinome [Manning 2002].
3. RDKit / Open-source cheminformatics overview.
4. AutoDock Vina [Trott 2010].
5. Connectivity Map [Lamb 2006] and LINCS [Subramanian 2017].
6. Random forest QSAR vs deep learning [Mayr 2018].
7. Chemprop [Yang 2019].
8. nnU-Net [Isensee 2021] — even off-topic, a masterclass in benchmarking.
9. AlphaFold [Jumper 2021].
10. ESM [Rives 2021; Lin 2023].
11. PCSK9 genetic story [Cohen 2006 + Sabatine 2017].
12. KRAS G12C [Canon 2019].
13. Ultra-large docking [Lyu 2019].
14. REINVENT 4 [Loeffler 2024].
15. AlphaFold3 [Abramson 2024].
16. PrimeKG + TxGNN [Chandak 2023; Huang 2024].
17. ADMET-AI [Swanson 2024].
18. Activity cliffs / MoleculeACE [van Tilborg 2022].
19. Chemical probes [SGC].
20. Genetics for drug targets [Nelson 2015 + Minikel 2024].
21. Open Targets [Ochoa 2021].
22. PoseBusters [Buttenschoen 2023].
23. DiffDock [Corso 2023].
24. CNS MPO [Wager 2010].
25. FEP+ [Wang 2015].

After 6 months you should be able to read any drug-discovery preprint with fluency. After 12, you should be able to spot the methodological weaknesses fast.
