# Targets, drugs, and atlases

> The lookup resources you'll keep returning to.

## Target / gene encyclopaedias

- **Open Targets** — integrated target-disease-drug evidence.
- **IDG TCRD** — Target Central Resource Database; tractability classification.
- **canSAR** — cancer-leaning target knowledgebase.
- **UniProt** — protein annotations.
- **GeneCards** — quick gene-level overview.

## Drug encyclopaedias

- **DrugBank** — curated drug entries, target/MOA/indication.
- **ChEMBL Drug Indications** — drug-disease links.
- **DailyMed** (NIH) — current US drug labels.
- **EMC** (electronic medicines compendium, UK).

## Disease ontologies

- **OMIM** — Mendelian inheritance.
- **MONDO** — unified disease ontology.
- **HPO** — Human Phenotype Ontology.
- **DisGeNET** — gene-disease associations.

## Pathway / network resources

- **Reactome, KEGG, WikiPathways** — curated pathways.
- **STRING, BioGRID, BioPlex** — protein-protein interactions.
- **OmniPath** — signed signalling network.

## Single-cell atlases

- **Human Cell Atlas** — major effort to map human cell types.
- **Tabula Sapiens / Muris** — multi-organ scRNA-seq atlases.
- **Allen Brain Atlas** — brain-specific.

## Chemical probes

- **SGC Chemical Probes Portal** — high-quality chemical probes per target.
- **Probes & Drugs** — curated chemical probe database.

## In practice

- **Open Targets** for target-disease evidence; almost always step one.
- **UniProt** for protein sequence and feature lookup.
- **DrugBank** for "is there an approved drug for this target?" questions.
- **SGC for chemical probes** before any chemical biology experiment.

This is a *lookup* chapter — bookmark the URLs; you'll be back.
